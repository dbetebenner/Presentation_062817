The ESSA Era

  • How has student growth changed in the last 15 years?
  • Not much (IMHO)
  • Yes, uses cases for student growth are different today.
  • Yes, analytics have advanced.
  • But there is still poor understanding of what student growth is and does.

Myriad Problems

  • Inconsistent and contradictory terminology.
  • Improperly used concepts/words/phrases.
  • Use of the results for high stakes accountability has only increased the confusion.
  • It doesn't have to be this hard.

Solution

  • How can we make student growth "simple" to understand?
  • We need better examples/analogies from outside of education to help users understand student achievement over time.
    • 401K Account planning and balance over time.
    • Weight loss plan and monitoring over time.

Growth Basics

  • We are governed by the rules of time:
    • There is a past.
    • There is a present.
    • There is a future.
  • Examining the past: Diagnosis
  • Examining the future: Prognosis

401K Planning

  • It's 2017 and you're 35.
  • You want to retire at 65 with $1,000,000 (in 2047 dollars).
  • You sit down with a financial adviser to map out a 401K contribution plan.
  • Assumptions: $0 at start, 7% return on investments, and 3% rate of inflation.
  • One million adjusted for inflation of 3% will be $2,427,262 in 30 years.
  • You will need to save $24,015 annually to reach that goal by age 65.

401K Year 1

  • Suppose at the end of Year 1 you saved $24,000.
  • You visit your adviser and she says you're "on track" to reach your financial goal.
  • Diagnosis (looking at the past year): OK.
  • Prognosis (looking forward): Adjust parameters and update annual contribution goals accordingly.

401K Year 2

  • Suppose at the end of Year 2 you have $35,000 and saved $15,000 in year 2.
  • You visit your adviser and she says you're "not on track" to reach your financial goal.
  • Diagnosis (looking at the past year):
    • Not so good when judged against long term goal (criterion-referenced).
    • $15,000 is still a lot more than most 36 year olds save (norm-referenced).
  • Prognosis (looking forward): Now have some catching up to do.
    • Lots of time so don't have to panic.
    • Adjust parameters and update annual contribution goals accordingly.
    • Possibly schedule a semi-annual check-up.

Associated Questions

  • All we've done is described the states of affairs tacking back and forth between diagnosis and prognosis.
    • We haven't made a prediction about whether you will reach your retirement goals.
    • We haven't evaluated the effectiveness of your financial adviser.
  • Student growth has been mostly concerned with questions analogous to whether the financial adviser is effective.
  • Education data can be used for much more than just the evaluation of schools and teachers.

Diagnostic Growth Uses

  • Diagnosis
    • Students with low growth can be flagged for possible remediation.
  • Prognosis
    • Students needing high growth to reach desirable outcomes should receive enrichment and more frequent monitoring.
